## What is a retrospective?

A retrospective is a review process most commonly used in businesses to review the outcomes of a given project. The general format is to answer each of the three following questions:

1. What went well?
2. What didn‚Äôt go well?
3. What will we change going forward?

The exact format varies between teams, but this is the format I‚Äôm most familiar with, and the format we‚Äôll use today.

## I‚Äôve been missing a key part of the process.

In reading [last year‚Äôs retrospective](/2017-personal-retrospective), I realized that I started with more or less a clean slate. I looked at 2017 as a whole, and completely ignored the fact that I‚Äôd set goals for 2017.

**This was a mistake. Why set goals at all if I‚Äôm not going to hold myself accountable?**

## What will I change going forward?

Using the good and the bad from 2018, I need to make an action plan for 2019.

But first, I want to change up the format a bit. A year is a really long time, and it‚Äôs very hard for me to know what my life will look like in 12 months. I mean, in general I think I‚Äôll be relatively stable this year in terms of living and working, but who knows if I‚Äôd actually be able to stick to a year of guitar lessons or if I can write 25 blog posts for the year?

Instead, **I‚Äôm going to do 90-day check-ins. A business practice I really love is the idea of short, targeted projects that aim toward a larger goal.** This approach makes great progress toward long-term goals, but provides a shorter feedback loop and the opportunity to reassess things periodically to make sure the long-term goal still makes sense.

What this means in practice is that I'll set a [high-level goal for the year, then create low-level, tactical goals](/level-setting) 90-days at a time.

### High-level 2019 goals.

In 2019, I have a few high-level goals:

- **I want to be more creative _off_ the computer.** I don‚Äôt really care how, but I‚Äôd like to develop new hobbies that don‚Äôt involve looking at a screen.

- **I want to double down on community.** I feel best when the work I do makes the people around me more successful, happier, more empowered, or otherwise a little bit better off than they were before I met them. I want this theme to inform all of my decisions in 2019.

### 90-day goals for Q1 2019.

My strategy for this year is to set short-term goals that push toward the high-level goals, but that require less commitment and are easy to adjust if, after three months, I feel like they‚Äôre not actually the right goals.
